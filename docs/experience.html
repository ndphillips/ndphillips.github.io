Experience

Education

PhD, Cognitive Psychology, University of Basel

I obtained a PhD in Cognitive Psychology from the University of Basel in Basel, Switzerland and the Max Planck Institute for Human Development in Berlin, Germany within the Center for Adaptive Rationality lead by Dr. Ralph Hertwig. The focus of my PhD research was on adaptive decision making rules in individuals and groups. See Phillips, Hertwig, Kareev & Avrahami, 2014 for an example publication from my PhD.

Masters, Quantitative Psychology, Ohio University

Prior to my PhD, I obtained a Masters in Psychology at Ohio University at the department of Social Judgment and Behavioral Decision making under Dr. Claudia Gonzalez-Vallejo. The focus of my Master’s thesis was on the role of experiences and expert advice on decisions.

Bachelors, Mathematics, Grinnell College

I obtained BA in Mathematics from Grinnell College. My undergraduate coursework focused on probability and statistics and behavioral decision making.

Experience

Academia

Pre-Doctoral Researcher, Ohio University

As a pre-doctoral researcher at Ohio University, I taught multiple university courses in statistics and research methods for psychology undergraduate students.

Pre-Doctoral Researcher, University of Basel

As a pre-doctoral researcher at the University of Basel, I taught undergraduate and masters level seminars in research methods.

Researcher, University of Konstanz

Publishing an e-book on statistical programming with R

I converted my teaching materials on statistical programming with R into a free e-book “YaRrr! The Pirate’s Guide to R” that is still used by students and researchers around the world

Following the completion of my PhD, I completed a research position at at the University of Konstanz in Konstanz, Germany in the department of Social Psychology and Decision Sciences lead by Dr. Wolfgang Gaissmaier. There I taught Bachelors, Masters, and PhD-level courses in statistical programming with R, and decision making. I ultimately translated my statistical programming teaching materials into a free e-book “YaRrr! The Pirate’s Guide to R”. The book was, and still is, used by many students and researchers around the world to learn statistical programming with R. The book is still available at https://bookdown.org/ndphillips/YaRrr/.

Industry

Here is a selection of my work experience. See my LinkedIn page for a more detailed history.

CTO & Co-Founder, Plinth Analytics

From $0 to $1M in 1.5 years of bootstrapped development

In just 1.5 years, we went from two people with an idea to a team of 5 and $1M in revenue across multiple pharma and healthtech clients.

Most recently, I was the CTO and Co-Founder of Plinth Analytics, a company that helps healthcare organizations make better decisions with messy healthcare data. In this role, I led the development of our data science platform and worked with our customers to understand their needs and deliver solutions that helped reduce costs and generate new use-cases for EHR, claims and genomic data.

I am currently a Quantitative Scientist at Flatiron Health, where I work on real-world data science problems in oncology.
